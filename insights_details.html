10 points for Entrepreneurs looking to complete projects.

  • Vishal Kodikal
  • 16 june 2018
  • 3 Min

I have seen many great concepts remain as great concepts. Many Entrepreneurs by their very nature often move onto new concepts, ideas and angles without completing the fundamental framework to their business. I have put together 10 points to keep in mind.

From setting up operations across Africa, Far East and Middle East to cracking down on illicit trade, having a project approach facilitated success in the field. I have attempted to highlight some key thoughts that , I believe , helped me to face challenges, overcome hurdles, leading to achievement of our goals, objectives and vision. Moreover, the greatest benefit to me , personally has been the perpetuation of a good relationship from my team members and all other protagonists across countries.

1. Create Manageable Goals

Rather than seeing your project as one task, break the project down into a number of micro goals and set the corresponding time frame for each of the deliverable. Ensure that you focus on quality control for each one of these micro goals and you will be amazed at the efficiency of progress.

2. Collaboration

You may be the sole proprietor of the vision of the project, but that doesn’t mean that it needs to stay in that realm until completion. Bring in other thinkers to help bring clarity and new momentum. No man is an Island and this is certainly true of entrepreneurs.

3. Procrastination

Commit to timeframes and remove non essential distractions. Power hours with the music cranked have proven to be far more effective than sitting in front of your workstation for hours and hours at a time.

4. Expect problems and embrace them

Almost all projects will hit an issue along the way. It sometimes seems rational to stay at that problem until its solved. Sometimes these problems just need time and a new perspective. Do not be stumped by small issues, if they cannot be solved immediately leave them and revalue towards the end of the project.

5. Revisit

Momentum is critical when completing a project. However, it can be easy to become careless when pushing through. Make sure that you revisit your previous work and conclusions and double check them. Make sure that you make a full plan at the start of the project, are you still on track with the initial concept?

6. Be realistic.

One of the major factors in project not being completed is that they run out of steam half way through. When looking at the proposed project or business, really question if this is something you want to dedicate this large period of time to. Many entrepreneurs simply become bored throughout the project. If you are passionate and believe in it, you will have a far greater chance of seeing it through. Avoid wasting large amounts of time by really committing to longevity to a project.

7. Enjoy it

When taking on a major project or new venture, the exciting time is the creative stage. The imagination of the success rather than the method of achieving it. However, a major issue in completion of projects is that the entrepreneurs stay in that imaginative state and cut corners in the delivery. Embrace the delivery and enjoy it, and set your routine around your interests. Give yourself flexibility in how you do the nitty gritties and it will start to become an adventure rather than a to do list. If you enjoy your work, you will be far more successful.

8. Perfectionism is a waste of time

How many hours can you waste in making sure that something is ‘just right’ only to then go back and change something after anyway. Unless your sector requires complete precision, understand than the perfect solution is sometimes subjective to your current mood and emotion and is changeable over time. Do not waste additional hours and days on the tiniest details if it is potentially going to change at a later date.

9. Celebrate progress

When you are in the very darkest details of the delivery of your project, it is easy to become lost, disillusioned and distracted on just how far you have come. Make sure you celebrate each completed task, and take time away from the project if necessary.

10. Don’t force it if its not right.

Sometimes things just don’t work out how we intended them to. The great thing I have learned from working with entrepreneurs is that new ideas seem to come on an almost daily basis. If it is really not working, and no progress seems apparent, sometimes you have to walk away and try something new. Appreciate why this has happened, learn from it and attack the next concept.
